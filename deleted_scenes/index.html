SCENE 7 · TAKE 1 · INT. APARTMENT · FRIDAY
This scene was cut because nothing happens.
The character was supposed to go out. They didn't.
They made tea. They read.
They felt, for a few hours, like themselves.
DIRECTOR'S COMMENTARY
"The editor wanted this cut. Nothing dramatic happens. But every person who saw the rough cut said this was their favorite scene. The editor still cut it. I've regretted it since."

SCENE 0 · TAKE 1 · THE OTHER PATH
This scene exists in an alternate version of the film that was never made.
In it, the character takes the job in the other city.
Everything after that is different.
Not better. Not worse. Different.
DIRECTOR'S COMMENTARY
"The cast for this version: identical. The character is the same person in different circumstances. The interesting thing — and the thing that made us cut it — is that they seem equally themselves in both versions."
SCENE 11 · THE LAST GOOD DAY
This scene was not labeled as important during filming.
It was an ordinary Tuesday.
The character had no idea it was the last ordinary Tuesday before everything changed.
So they treated it like an ordinary Tuesday.
If they had known: they would have looked up more.
They didn't know.
DIRECTOR'S COMMENTARY
"We almost didn't include this in the deleted scenes. It's too quiet. But every screening — someone comes up afterward and says: that was the one. That was the scene."
