ISSUE 1: RESERVATION POLICY

Affirmative action through quotas in education and public employment to address historical injustices vs concerns about merit and systemic efficiency.

FOR RESERVATION

  • Historical caste-based oppression requires structural, not just formal, equality.
  • Representation in bureaucracy and power structures ensures marginalized voices are heard.
  • "Merit" is often a function of accumulated social and economic capital, not just raw intelligence.

FOR REFORM

  • Benefits often concentrate among the "creamy layer" rather than reaching the most marginalized.
  • The policy creates caste-based political mobilization rather than caste annihilation.
  • Perpetual extension of quotas without exit strategies defeats the original constitutional intent.

FOR ALTERNATIVES

  • Economic deprivation criteria (EWS) are more relevant than caste in modern India.
  • Focus should shift to capacity building: better primary schools, health, and equal opportunity at birth.
  • Quotas in specialized technical/medical fields may compromise public safety and efficiency.
DATA: Less than 5% of all jobs in India are in the public sector where reservations apply.

People you respect disagree on this. This is why.

ISSUE 2: UNIFORM CIVIL CODE

Replacing personal laws based on the scriptures and customs of each major religious community in India with a common set of rules governing every citizen.

FOR UCC

  • Ensures gender equality, as many personal laws are patriarchal.
  • Fulfills Article 44 of the Constitution's Directive Principles.
  • Secularism requires the state to separate religion from civil law.

FOR NUANCE

  • A "Uniform" code might just impose majority Hindu laws on minorities.
  • Tribal customs and regional diversities require legal accommodation.
  • Piecemeal reform of existing personal laws might be less socially disruptive.

AGAINST UCC

  • Violates Freedom of Religion (Article 25) to practice distinct customs.
  • Pluralism and legal diversity are strengths of the Indian civilization.
  • Minorities perceive it as an attempt at cultural assimilation.
DATA: Goa has implemented a form of UCC since Portuguese rule, with specific exemptions.

ISSUE 3: ECONOMIC LIBERALIZATION

The 1991 reforms and the continuing debate over free markets, privatization, and wealth distribution.

PRO-MARKET

  • Deregulation unleashed high GDP growth and pulled millions out of extreme poverty.
  • Private capital is more efficient than inefficient Public Sector Undertakings.

WELFARE FOCUS

  • Growth has been "jobless", exacerbating inequality.
  • The state must provide safety nets (MGNREGA, PDS) as markets fail the poorest.

STRUCTURAL CRITIQUE

  • Liberalization led to crony capitalism and concentration of wealth in few conglomerates.
  • Labor laws and environmental regulations are sacrificed for corporate profit.
DATA: India's top 1% hold over 40% of the national wealth.

ISSUE 4: ENVIRONMENT VS DEVELOPMENT

India's development needs vs its 2070 net-zero commitment and ecological preservation.

DEVELOPMENT FIRST

  • India's per capita emissions are far below the global average; the West caused the crisis.
  • Millions still lack reliable electricity; cheap coal is necessary for short-term poverty alleviation.

ECOLOGY FIRST

  • India is highly vulnerable to climate change (monsoon disruption, heatwaves).
  • Ecological destruction for infrastructure leads to long-term economic loss (floods, soil depletion).

Both concerns are legitimate. The policy must navigate both.

ISSUE 5: URBAN-RURAL DIVIDE

Policy choices that favor cities vs rural areas in a nation where 65% live in villages.

URBAN FOCUS

  • Cities are engines of economic growth and innovation.
  • Urbanization is inevitable; policy should facilitate smooth transition rather than artificially holding people in agriculture.

RURAL FOCUS

  • Agricultural distress causes mass migration; we must make village life economically viable.
  • Disproportionate capital investment in cities creates unlivable megacities and neglected hinterlands.

Urban India votes differently than rural India. Policy reflects this.

ISSUE 6: FREEDOM OF SPEECH

The balance between Article 19(1)(a) individual expression and Article 19(2) reasonable restrictions.

MAXIMALIST

  • Speech should only be restricted if it directly incites violence.
  • "Offense" is subjective; laws against hurting religious sentiments are weaponized.

BALANCED

  • In a volatile, diverse democracy, hate speech can trigger deadly riots rapidly.
  • Platform regulation is necessary against coordinated misinformation.

STATE SECURITY

  • National integrity supersedes individual rights in border states or insurgency areas.
  • Foreign interference through digital platforms requires strict data sovereignty.
DATA: India leads the world in internet shutdowns.
