WHY TOPSOIL DIES

MONO-CROPPING

Same crop yearly destroys microbial diversity. Soil becomes dependent on inputs.

CHEMICAL OVERUSE

Kills beneficial organisms, compacts soil, destroys the natural nutrient cycle.

OVERGRAZING

Removes cover, reduces root depth, causes severe wind and water erosion.

BARE SOIL

Without plant cover, sun bakes the soil biology and rain washes away the A-horizon.

SOIL BIODIVERSITY

EARTHWORM

The earthworm processes dead matter into nutrition.

MYCORRHIZAL FUNGI

The fungi connect plants across meters — a literal underground internet.

NITROGEN-FIXING BACTERIA

Pull nitrogen from the air and make it available to plant roots.

NEMATODES

Microscopic predators that cycle nutrients back into the root zone.

REGENERATIVE PRACTICES

COVER CROPPING

Plants always cover soil. Protects from erosion, feeds microbes, adds organic matter.

COMPOSTING

Returns organic matter. Inoculates dead soil with the soil food web biology.

CROP ROTATION

Different crops per season. Maintains diversity, disrupts pest cycles naturally.

NO-TILL FARMING

Don't break soil structure. Let fungi networks grow undisturbed.

BIOCHAR

Ancient technique. Locks carbon in soil for centuries, provides housing for microbes.
