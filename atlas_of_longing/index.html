Departure

41° 53' N, 12° 28' E
We left before the city woke. The harbor was black glass. I did not look back at the shoreline, terrified that if I saw it shrinking, I might ask the captain to turn around. We sail for the blank spaces on the map.

The Doldrums

0° 0' N, 23° 45' W
The wind died thirteen days ago. The sea is thick as oil. The men do not speak anymore; they only stare at the horizon, waiting for a line to break the endless blue. Time has stopped moving forward. It only pools around us.

Isle of Ash

15° 11' S, 45° 12' E
We found land, but it is entirely black sand and smoking vents. Nothing grows here. We walked for hours and found only silence. It is as if the earth itself had burned out. We leave tomorrow. The men are afraid.

The Leviathan

48° 52' S, 123° 23' W
At midnight, the ship lurched. I ran to the deck. Beneath the hull, a pale shape larger than our vessel was gliding silently. It glowed with a cold, ghostly light. It did not attack. It only observed us, and then descended into the abyss. We are not alone in the deep.
